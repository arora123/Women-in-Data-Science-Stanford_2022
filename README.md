🧠 Talk Overview

Charts are everywhere; in newspapers, social media, reports, presentations, and dashboards. Yet many of them unintentionally (or intentionally) mislead through hidden biases, distorted scales, missing context, or overloaded information.

In this talk at **WiDS Silicon Valley 2022**, I break down common ways visualizations can deceive and explain how to critically evaluate charts before trusting them. I also share practical guidelines for creating honest, clear, and responsible visualizations.

The session is tool-agnostic and relevant whether you use Excel, R, Python, Power BI, Tableau, D3, or any other visualization platform.

The goal is to build visual literacy, so you can both detect misleading charts and avoid creating them.
